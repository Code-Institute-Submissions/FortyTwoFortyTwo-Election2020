Presidential Election

Electoral College

United States held the 59th presidential election on Tuesday November 3rd 2020. With a record high voter turnout for 120 years, Joe Biden won aganist the incumbent Donald Trump. Trump became the first president to lose re-election since George H.W. Bush in 1992. There are 538 electoral votes aviable with 270 required for a majority, Biden carried 306 votes while Trump carried 232 votes. That result is the exact opposite of the 2016 result where Trump won aganist Hillary Clinton.

Congressional District

Each of the 50 states and Washington DC have their own different rules and laws, including Nebraska and Maine which awards electoral votes by congressional district instead of winner takes all. 1 electoral vote awarded for each congressional district, and 2 remaining electoral votes for statewide. In Nebraska, Trump won statewide, 1st and 3rd congressional district with 4 electoral votes, while Biden won 2nd congressional district with 1 electoral vote. In Maine, Biden won statewide and 1st congressional district with 3 electoral votes, while Trump won 2nd congressional district with 1 electoral vote.

Timeline

Early voting begins in several states on September 20th, 45 days before Election Day. During November 3rd election night, there is no clear winner until 4 days later, November 7th when many experts and major news outlets projected Joe Biden as the winner. Election results in each 50 states and District of Columbia were certified by December 9th. All 538 presidential electors formally cast their votes for president and vice president on December 14, and their votes will be officially counted by Congress on January 6, 2021. President-elect Joe Biden and Vice President-elect Kamala Harris are scheduled to be inaugurated on January 20, 2021.
